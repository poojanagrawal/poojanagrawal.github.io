I am a PhD candidate at the Centre for Astrophysics and Supercomputing, Swinburne University of Technology, Melbourne, Australia. I am also part of the ARC Centre of Excellence for Gravitational Wave Discovery (OzGrav) and the Astronomical Society of Australia.

Growing up in a small city in India, the night sky always fascinated me. I wanted to know everything about the stars and my curiosity has led me to places further than I ever imagined.

I earned a Bachelor of Science degree from University of Lucknow, where I learned the basics of stellar astronomy along with maths and physics. I went on to obtain a Masters of Science degree in physics with a specialization in astronomy & astrophysics from Savitribai Phule Pune University. I was awarded the Prof. W. M. Dabadghav gold medal and the Prof. S. S. Joshi Prize for securing highest marks in the course.

While pursuing my undergraduate degree from Lucknow, I became part of the local amateur astronomer's club. There, I learnt to use telescopes and developed an interest in communicating science to the public. Over-time, I have found myself increasingly involved in science communication. In 2019, I received the director's excellence in outreach and outreach superstar awards for my outreach activities during my PhD.

When I am not working on curating stellar lives or developing resources to communicate it, I find myself busy in a wide range of activities. I enjoy painting, writing poems, watching movies, and exploring places - capturing moments with a camera whenever I can. During the great lockdown of 2020, I have also developed the hobby of reading books and playing board games.
